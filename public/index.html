Mom, I Love You!

Stella Comeaux

Stella Comeaux - New Iberia, Louisiania

This song is a testament to the enduring light of Stella Comeaux, my mother whose wealth was never measured in dollars, but in the boundless love she poured into her four children. From her roots in a hardworking Cajun family to the quiet strength she showed as a single mother, she moved through life with a "Grand Champion" spirit. Whether she was working long, painful hours to provide or patrolling the halls of her final home with a "guard on duty" smile, Stella remained a beacon of generosity and joy. This melody captures her journey from the dusty fields of her youth to the peaceful, paid-for home where so many memories were made, ensuring her legacy as a caregiver and best friend is never forgotten.

The music itself carries the rhythm of her heritage, blending the soulful, rolling piano of a New Orleans shuffle with the spirited cry of a Cajun fiddle. It is a celebration of the simple, beautiful moments—like a grandfather sharing a vegetable cut in two—and the deep, sincere hugs that defined Stella’s presence. Though the years have passed, this tribute serves as a bridge to the past, sending "lots of kisses" to the sky for a woman who was truly one of a kind. No one can take away the thoughts of her warm smile, and through these verses, the champion of the Bayou continues to wheel through our hearts with delight.
